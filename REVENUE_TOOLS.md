## 1. Payment Gateway for KSA

### Stripe - NOT Available Natively

Stripe does not officially support Saudi Arabia. Workarounds exist (form a US LLC or UK Ltd, get an EIN, open a foreign bank account) but this adds complexity, cost, and compliance risk. **Not recommended as primary gateway.**

### Recommended: Tap Payments (Primary)

**Why Tap wins for this business:**
- Built for KSA, licensed by SAMA
- **goCollect** app: send branded payment links via WhatsApp, SMS, Instagram, email -- perfect for invoicing brands directly
- Supports Mada, Visa, Mastercard, Amex, Apple Pay, STC Pay
- Instant account setup and activation
- Multi-currency acceptance (critical for international brand deals)
- PCI compliant, 3D Secure on all cards
- Dashboard with payment tracking and reporting

**Fees:**
- Mada transactions: 1% (capped at SAR 200)
- International cards: ~2.85% + 0.30 SAR per transaction

**Website:** https://www.tap.company/en-sa

### Backup: Moyasar

**Why keep as backup:**
- Saudi-based (Riyadh), licensed by SAMA
- First Saudi gateway to support Samsung Pay
- PCI DSS Level 1 compliant, eMSP certified
- Supports Mada, Visa, Mastercard, Amex, Apple Pay, Samsung Pay
- Integrated dashboard with business intelligence

**Fees:**
- Mada: ~1.75% + 1 SAR per transaction
- International cards: ~2.2% - 2.9% + 1 SAR per transaction
- Custom pricing available based on volume

**Website:** https://moyasar.com/en/

### Other Options (Not Recommended as Primary)

| Provider | Fees | Notes |
|----------|------|-------|
| HyperPay | 2.5% - 3.5% | Enterprise-focused, overkill for talent management |
| PayTabs | ~2.7% + 1 SAR | Good reputation but less creator-friendly |

### Decision: Use Tap Payments

Tap's goCollect app is built exactly for this use case -- sending payment links to brands via WhatsApp/Instagram. No website integration needed. Brands click a link, pay, done.

## 2. Invoicing Solution

### Recommended: Zoho Invoice (Primary)

**Why Zoho Invoice:**
- Free tier available for small businesses
- **Arabic language support** (required by ZATCA for Saudi e-invoices)
- ZATCA e-invoicing compliant (Phase 2)
- Auto-generates QR codes and e-signatures required by ZATCA
- Bulk push invoices to ZATCA portal for authentication
- Professional invoice templates
- Multi-currency support (USD, EUR, GBP, SAR)
- Credit/debit notes for amendments
- Client portal for brands to view and pay invoices

**Website:** https://www.zoho.com/sa/invoice/

### Alternative: FreshBooks

**Why consider:**
- Time tracking built in (useful for hourly consulting fees)
- Project management features
- Professional templates
- Strong client management

**Pricing:** Starting at $19/month

**Downside:** No native Arabic support, not ZATCA-compliant out of the box.

### Alternative: Wave (Free)

**Pros:** Completely free, unlimited invoices
**Cons:** No Arabic support, no ZATCA compliance, limited to basic invoicing

### Decision: Use Zoho Invoice

ZATCA compliance is non-negotiable for a Saudi business. Zoho Invoice handles Arabic invoicing, e-invoicing requirements, and multi-currency -- all for free at the small business tier. Pair it with Tap Payments goCollect for payment collection.

**Workflow:**
1. Create invoice in Zoho Invoice (Arabic + English, ZATCA compliant)
2. Send payment link via Tap goCollect (WhatsApp/email/SMS)
3. Brand pays via link (Mada/Visa/Mastercard/Apple Pay)
4. Track payment status in both Zoho and Tap dashboards
5. Zoho auto-pushes e-invoice to ZATCA for authentication

## 3. Contract Templates Needed

### A. Brand Partnership Agreement

**Must include:**
- Scope of work (platforms, number of posts, content type)
- Deliverables and timeline (draft review dates, posting dates)
- Compensation structure (flat fee, performance bonus, product gifting)
- Payment terms (50% upfront / 50% on delivery, NET 30, etc.)
- Content approval process (number of revision rounds)
- Exclusivity clause (competitor restrictions, duration)
- Cancellation/termination terms
- FTC/Saudi disclosure requirements (influencer must disclose partnership)

### B. Content License Agreement

**Must include:**
- License type (exclusive vs. non-exclusive)
- Duration of license (6 months, 1 year, perpetual)
- Territories (KSA only, GCC, global)
- Permitted platforms (brand's social, website, paid ads, OOH)
- Sublicensing rights (can the brand give content to a third party?)
- Pricing tiers:
  - Social media repost: base rate
  - Website usage: 1.5x base
  - Paid advertising: 2-3x base
  - Out-of-home / print: 3-5x base
  - Perpetual / buyout: 5-10x base

### C. Usage Rights Extension

**Must include:**
- Original agreement reference
- Extended duration and territories
- Additional fee for extension
- Updated exclusivity terms

### D. Talent Management Agreement (Between Hussa and the Influencer)

**Must include:**
- Commission structure (standard: 15-20% of brand deals)
- Duration of representation
- Exclusive vs. non-exclusive representation
- Scope of services (negotiation, invoicing, content strategy, brand outreach)
- Termination clause (notice period, post-termination commission tail)
- Expense handling

### Where to Get Templates

- **Foundd Legal** (founddlegal.com) -- influencer-specific contract templates
- **LawDepot** (lawdepot.com) -- customizable influencer contract templates
- **Ironclad** -- enterprise contract management
- **ISBA** (isba.org.uk) -- industry-standard influencer contract templates
- Have a Saudi lawyer review all templates for local law compliance

## 4. Tax Considerations for Saudi Content Creators

### VAT (Value Added Tax) - 15%

**Mandatory registration threshold:** SAR 375,000/year (~$100,000 USD)
- If annual taxable income exceeds SAR 375,000, must register within 30 days
- Once registered, charge 15% VAT on all Saudi-based services

**Voluntary registration threshold:** SAR 187,500 - SAR 375,000/year
- Optional but recommended for:
  - Recovering input VAT on business expenses
  - Establishing market credibility with brands

**Below SAR 187,500/year:** No VAT registration required

### E-Invoicing (Fatoora)

ZATCA mandates electronic invoicing for all businesses:
- **Phase 1:** Generate and store e-invoices electronically (already in effect)
- **Phase 2:** Integration with ZATCA portal for real-time authentication
- Invoices must include: Arabic text, QR code, e-signature, VAT number
- Zoho Invoice handles all of this automatically

### Zakat

- Saudi-owned businesses pay 2.5% Zakat on net worth annually
- Filed through ZATCA portal
- Applies to both companies and sole proprietors

### No Personal Income Tax

Saudi Arabia has **no personal income tax**. Revenue from content creation and talent management is subject to:
- VAT (if above threshold)
- Zakat (2.5% on net worth)
- No capital gains tax
- No withholding tax on domestic payments

### Key Action Items

1. Register on ZATCA portal (zatca.gov.sa)
2. Get a Tax Identification Number (TIN)
3. Monitor revenue against SAR 375,000 threshold
4. Use ZATCA-compliant invoicing (Zoho Invoice)
5. File quarterly VAT returns (within 30 days of period end)
6. Consult a Saudi tax advisor for Zakat obligations

## 5. Accepting International Payments (USD, EUR, GBP)

### The Challenge

Saudi businesses receive payouts in SAR by default. SAMA enforces currency controls. International brands want to pay in their local currency.

### Solution Stack

**For International Brands (USD/EUR/GBP):**

1. **Tap Payments goCollect** -- send payment links that accept international cards in multiple currencies. Tap handles conversion and settles to your Saudi bank account in SAR.

2. **Wise Business Account** (wise.com) -- open a multi-currency business account to hold USD, EUR, GBP balances. Receive international wire transfers with local bank details in each currency. Convert to SAR at mid-market rates when needed.

3. **PayPal Business** -- widely used by international brands. Accept payments in any currency. Higher fees (~3.5% + fixed fee) but universally recognized.

**For Saudi/GCC Brands (SAR):**

1. **Tap Payments** -- Mada + local cards, lowest fees (1% for Mada)
2. **Direct bank transfer** -- provide IBAN for large deals

### Recommended Setup

| Brand Location | Payment Method | Currency | Fee |
|---------------|---------------|----------|-----|
| Saudi / GCC | Tap goCollect (Mada) | SAR | 1% |
| Saudi / GCC | Bank transfer | SAR | Free |
| International | Tap goCollect (Visa/MC) | USD/EUR/GBP | ~2.85% |
| International | Wise Business | USD/EUR/GBP | ~0.5-1.5% |
| International | PayPal Business | Any | ~3.5% |

### For Large International Deals ($10K+)

Use direct wire transfer to a Wise Business account (local USD/EUR/GBP bank details). Lower fees than card processing. Provide the brand with a Zoho Invoice and Wise bank details.

## 6. Payment Link Setup

### Tap goCollect -- Step by Step

1. **Download goCollect app** (iOS / Android)
2. **Create account** with Saudi business details (CR number or freelance certificate)
3. **Link bank account** (Saudi IBAN)
4. **Create a payment link:**
   - Set amount (SAR or foreign currency)
   - Add description (e.g., "Hussa AlSaif x [Brand] - Instagram Campaign Q2 2026")
   - Set expiry date
   - Add brand logo for branded experience
5. **Send via any channel:**
   - WhatsApp (most common for Saudi brands)
   - Email (for international brands)
   - SMS
   - Instagram DM
6. **Brand clicks link** --> sees branded payment page --> pays with card/Apple Pay/Mada
7. **Get notified** when payment hits
8. **Auto-settlement** to bank account

### Payment Link Best Practices

- Always send the Zoho Invoice first (for records/ZATCA), then follow with the Tap payment link
- Set payment link expiry to 7-14 days
- Enable automatic reminders in goCollect
- For recurring brand partnerships, set up recurring payment links
- Keep payment descriptions consistent for bookkeeping

### Alternative: Moyasar Payment Links

Moyasar also supports payment links if you need a backup. Same flow -- create link, send to brand, they pay. Settlement to Saudi bank account.
