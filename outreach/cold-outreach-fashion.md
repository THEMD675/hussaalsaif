## CUSTOMIZATION NOTES

**Fashion/luxury brands require a different approach than beauty:**

1. **Lead with taste, not metrics.** Luxury brands care more about aesthetic alignment and brand safety than raw follower counts. Mention the curated 25-post Instagram feed -- it signals premium.
2. **Reference specific collections or moments.** "I loved the FW26 show" is weak. "The way [Brand Name] incorporated [specific detail] in the [specific collection] aligns with how my audience responds to [specific theme]" is strong.
3. **Position yourself as a peer, not a vendor.** Luxury brands work with people who understand the brand world. Your PR background and TEDx experience are differentiators here.
4. **Never mention rates in the first email.** Luxury brands have budgets. Let them come to you with a proposal after seeing the media kit.
5. **Timing matters.** Reach out 6-8 weeks before major fashion events, Ramadan campaigns, and Saudi National Day (September 23).

**Contact research approach for fashion/luxury:**
- LVMH brands: Contact LVMH Middle East PR team in Dubai (one team covers Fendi, Dior, Louis Vuitton, Celine, Loewe, Givenchy)
- Kering brands: Contact Kering ME for Gucci, Balenciaga, Saint Laurent, Bottega Veneta
- Richemont brands: Contact Richemont ME for Cartier, Van Cleef, Montblanc
- Saudi-specific: Contact **Niche Arabia** (Marriam Mossalli's agency) -- they represent 150+ luxury clients in KSA
- GCC retailers: Check the brand website for partnerships@ or PR@ email; search LinkedIn for "Influencer Marketing Manager" at the company
