## CUSTOMIZATION NOTES

**Lifestyle is the broadest category, so personalization is critical:**

1. **Be specific about which content format the brand fits into.** "My favorite time of the day" routine content, "what's in my bag" features, numbered vlogs, workout days, daily routines -- each has a different audience and vibe.

2. **Don't oversell lifestyle as secondary.** Frame it as an extension of the trust she's built, not a side hustle. Her audience follows HER, which means any category she features gets the same trust.

3. **Lean into the multidimensional angle.** Hussa is not a one-trick creator. PR professional + curly hair authority + book club host + lifestyle creator. For lifestyle brands, this breadth IS the value proposition.

4. **For fragrance brands:** Mention the Saudi/GCC gift culture and the importance of scent in the region. Fragrance is a massive category in the Gulf.

5. **For food/cafe brands:** Reference her "I don't know how to cook but I'm trying" content -- it's relatable and funny, and food brands can integrate naturally into that narrative.

6. **For fitness brands:** Reference the "workout + shopping days" content pattern. It's genuine and recurring.

**Contact research for lifestyle brands:**
- Fragrance (Jo Malone, Diptyque): Via parent company PR teams (Estee Lauder, LVMH) or ME distributors
- Fitness (Nike, Adidas, Lululemon): Search for "Influencer Marketing" or "Brand Partnerships" roles on LinkedIn for the ME division
- Tech: Via regional PR agencies -- most tech brands use agencies like Weber Shandwick, Edelman, or APCO Worldwide for ME PR
- Apps/platforms: Direct outreach to marketing@ or partnerships@ listed on their website
- Hotels/travel: PR agencies or hotel marketing managers on LinkedIn
